üìö Understanding the Visualizations

What are ORB and AKAZE?

  • ORB (Oriented FAST and Rotated BRIEF): A FAST detector that finds many keypoints quickly. Good for speed, finds corners and edges. Shows up as many small green circles (~25,000-50,000 points).
  • AKAZE (Accelerated-KAZE): A more sophisticated detector that finds fewer but more stable keypoints. Better for accuracy, finds distinctive features. Shows up as fewer larger circles (~5,000-20,000 points).

What Each Visualization Shows:

  • Original Images: The raw input photos before any processing
  • Keypoints (Green Circles): Points the algorithm thinks are interesting/matchable - like corners, edges, or unique patterns. Bigger circles = larger scale features.
  • Initial Matches (Lines): All potential correspondences between images - many are wrong!
  • RANSAC Filtered (Final Matches): Only the correct matches after removing outliers. These are used to compute how to align the images.

üí° Simple Explanation: The algorithm finds interesting points in both images (keypoints), matches similar ones between images (matching), then removes incorrect matches (RANSAC) to properly align and stitch them together.
